About Me

Hello, I am currently studying mathematics and computer science at Bakersfield College in Bakersfield, California.

I am also a veteran and served in the United States Marine Corps (OEF 13.1-13.2).

Now, I enjoy studying data and data science. Consequently, I am a huge fan of Python and Python's scientific computing libraries and packages, most of which fall under the Anacdona umbrella:

  • Numpy
  • Pandas
  • Jupyter Notebooks
  • Scikit Learn

In search of richer data visualizations I began to look into D3. If you Google anything remotely Javascript related you're probably one click away from Node.js.

The Node runtime makes it incredibly easy to get started on your web apps. However, a common refrain I continue to come across is it is "easy to get started, impossible to master" so I suppose I should buckle up.

More

I also use Flask, a micro framework for Python. I find it to be semantically similar to using Node.js and Express.js, more so than say, Django. However, I do understand they are fundamentally different.

These days it seems like the most valuable skill is adaptability, probably second only to speed. I have used Java, C++, Python, Javascript, HTML5, CSS.
